Nepal is a landlocked country in South Asia, situated mainly in the Himalayas but also including parts of the Indo-Gangetic Plain. It borders China to the north and India to the south, east, and west.

The country's geography is dominated by the Himalayas, the highest mountain range in the world. Eight of the world's ten tallest peaks lie within Nepal or on its border, including Mount Everest, the highest point on Earth above sea level.

Physiographic regions

Nepal is commonly divided into three physiographic belts: the Terai lowlands, the Hill region, and the high Mountain region. Each runs roughly east to west across the country.

The Koshi River, Gandaki River, and Karnali River systems drain the slopes, feeding eventually into the Ganges.
